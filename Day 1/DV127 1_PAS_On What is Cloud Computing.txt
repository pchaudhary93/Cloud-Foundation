1_SAS_On What is Cloud Computing -1.




	Q1
	What type of hardware do we need to build an application?
	Ans
	We will need servers, operating servers and license, firewall, routers, switches, load balancers, databases, etc. 


	Q2
	What is the difference between capital expenditure and operational expenditure?
	Ans
	Capital expenditures are major purchases a company makes that are designed to be used over the long-term. Operating expenses are day-to-day expenses a company incurs to keep their business operational. 


	Q3
	What type of resource do small businesses and companies have now that wasn’t available traditionally, where companies don’t have to invest in hardware? 
	Ans
	On-demand access to web-based applications, data storage, processing, and other services. 


	A4
	What are some examples of cloud providers?
	Ans
	The big three - Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, and Amazon Web Services (AWS) are considered the established leaders. 


	Q5
	What is the benefit of using the cloud?
	Ans
	The cloud delivers more flexibility and reliability, increased performance and efficiency, and helps to lower IT costs. 


	Q6
	What is the difference between initial traditional hardware setup and cloud?
	Ans
	Hardware is tangible and fixed, while cloud is intangible and flexible. Cloud computing relies on the internet and remote servers, while traditional computing uses local hardware and software. 


	Q7
	What is cloud computing?
	Ans
	Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services including servers, storages, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence. 


	Q8
	What is the benefit of using cloud computing?
	Ans
	Cloud computing gives your business more flexibility. 


	Q9
	Where can someone access cloud computing from?
	Ans
	Data that is stored in the cloud can be accessed by visiting the website or mobile application of the cloud services provider. 


	Q10
	What was the example given by the federal CIO on cloud computing?
	Ans
	The example was today, shared public utilities give us access to clean water by simply turning on the tap; cloud computing works in a similar fashion.
